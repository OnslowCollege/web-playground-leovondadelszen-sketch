Lebron James

Lebron James was born in 1984, in Ohio, U.S. from a young age he shone at basketball. First joining a team at 9 years old, he quickly caught the eye of scouts and coaches. Lebron Made his NBA debut in 2003 for the Cleveland Cavaliers, and in 2009, had a four year transfer to miami heat, winning them two championship titles for just before his departure. He then went back to the Cleveland Cavaliers for another four years, and won them their only championship title. Lebron then transfered to Los Angeles Lakers in 208, and is still there, currently with another championship title. Some of Lebron's other achievements include being a 19-time NBA All-Star, 4-time NBA MVP, and 4-time NBA Finals MVP. He is also known for his philanthropy work, including the LeBron James Family Foundation, which focuses on education and youth development. Lebron is widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time, and his impact on and off the court has been significant. As well as the fact that he is the all-time leading scorer in NBA history.
